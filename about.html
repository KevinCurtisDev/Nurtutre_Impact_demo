About Nurture Impact

Twenty Years of Experience. Now Working for You.

Meet Alan Curtis

For over twenty years, Alan worked at the heart of Ireland’s community funding landscape managing major Pobal programmes, reviewing thousands of applications, and seeing first-hand what separates successful submissions from the rest.

He’s sat on the other side of the appraisal table. He knows how applications are scored, where organisations lose marks, and what makes an appraiser say “yes.”

Alongside this funding expertise, Alan brings deep governance experience built over decades of board service, senior leadership, and sectoral stewardship. He has supported voluntary boards, charities, and social enterprises through restructures, compliance challenges, strategic planning, and organisational change. His governance approach is practical, empowering, and rooted in real-world experience of what good oversight looks like and what happens when it’s missing.

He understands the pressures facing boards today: regulatory expectations, risk management, financial oversight, board dynamics, and the need for clarity of roles and responsibilities. Alan has mentored countless board members and senior leaders, helping them strengthen governance systems, build confidence, and create organisations that are both compliant and mission‑driven.

Now, through Nurture Impact, Alan brings this combined expertise in funding, governance, and organisational development directly to the community groups, social enterprises, and voluntary boards who need it most supporting them to thrive, not just survive.

The Nurture Impact Approach

Alan founded Nurture Impact because he saw too many good organisations struggle with funding – not because their work wasn't valuable, but because they didn't know how to present it in a way that appraisers could score highly.

The same was true for governance. Boards wanted to do the right thing but felt overwhelmed by compliance requirements and jargon-heavy guidance.

Nurture Impact exists to bridge that gap. Every masterclass is built on a simple principle: practical skills that can be used immediately. No theory for theory's sake. No death by PowerPoint. Just clear, actionable guidance from someone who's been on both sides.

What Guides the Work

  • Clarity over complexity – If it can't be explained simply, it's not ready to teach
  • Tools, not just talk – Every session includes templates and resources participants can use straight away
  • Respect for the sector – Community organisations are doing vital work with limited resources; training should recognise that reality
  • Long-term capacity – The goal isn't dependency; it's helping organisations stand stronger on their own
